# Source Policy

Discounts are volatile and many are targeted. Browse or otherwise verify current details before recommending a specific offer.

## Source Priority

Prefer:

1. Issuer offer pages and official issuer help pages.
2. Merchant or brand promotion pages and terms.
3. Official shopping portals and portal terms.
4. Official loyalty program pages.
5. Reputable portal comparison tools for discovery, then verify with the portal.
6. Community datapoints only when clearly labeled as anecdotal.

Avoid relying on:

- Search-result snippets.
- Coupon pages with no merchant confirmation.
- Expired blog posts.
- Affiliate landing pages that obscure terms.
- User account screenshots unless the user provides non-sensitive text details.

## Targeted Offer Language

Use careful wording:

- "Check whether this appears in your Amex Offers / Chase Offers / Citi Merchant Offers / BankAmeriDeals account."
- "Public source confirms the program exists; availability is targeted."
- "Do not assume eligibility until the offer is activated in your account."

## Data To Capture

For each offer:

- Source URL.
- Last checked date.
- Offer name.
- Eligible issuer/card.
- Merchant.
- Discount type: statement credit, percentage back, fixed cash back, points multiplier, portal rate, coupon, gift card discount, loyalty reward.
- Minimum spend.
- Maximum discount or cap.
- Expiration date and timezone if available.
- Enrollment requirement.
- Online/in-store/app restrictions.
- Payment method restrictions.
- Exclusions.
- Stackability terms.

## Privacy

Never ask for:

- Full card numbers.
- CVV.
- Account passwords.
- One-time passcodes.
- Full billing address unless needed for a public merchant shipping rule, and then avoid retaining it.

Ask users to summarize targeted offers in plain text instead of sharing sensitive screenshots.
