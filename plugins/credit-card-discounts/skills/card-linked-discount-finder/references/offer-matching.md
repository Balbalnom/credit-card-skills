# Offer Matching

## Savings Types

Consider:

- Card-linked issuer offers.
- Card category bonuses.
- Statement credits.
- Shopping portal rates.
- Merchant coupon codes.
- Merchant loyalty rewards.
- Gift card discounts.
- Wallet or checkout offers.
- Shipping, return, and warranty value.
- Purchase protection or extended warranty.

## Stackability Checklist

Check whether each layer can stack:

1. Start from merchant sale price.
2. Add merchant coupon or loyalty promo if allowed.
3. Click through shopping portal if portal terms allow coupon use.
4. Activate issuer card-linked offer before purchase.
5. Pay with the eligible enrolled card.
6. Avoid digital wallets if terms require direct card payment.
7. Keep receipts and screenshots of activated terms when appropriate.

Flag conflicts:

- Portal disallows coupon codes not listed on portal.
- Issuer offer excludes gift cards.
- Offer requires direct merchant checkout, not third-party marketplace.
- In-store offer does not work online, or online offer excludes app orders.
- Statement credit requires one transaction, not cumulative spend.
- Split tender may void eligibility.

## Ranking

Rank by practical net value:

- Confirmed eligibility.
- Net dollar savings after caps.
- Probability of tracking.
- Return policy and warranty tradeoffs.
- Effort required.
- Expiration urgency.
- Whether using the offer sacrifices a better card category bonus.

## Reverse Matching

When the user gives cards and asks for brands:

- Group by issuer or card.
- Ask the user to verify targeted offers in each issuer account.
- Prioritize high-value, expiring, and frequently used merchants.
- Include categories where public portal or merchant offers are strong even without targeted issuer offers.

## Reminder Suggestions

Suggest reminders for:

- Offer expiration 7 days and 1 day before deadline.
- Quarterly category activation.
- Portal bonus expiration.
- High-value merchant promos during planned purchase windows.
