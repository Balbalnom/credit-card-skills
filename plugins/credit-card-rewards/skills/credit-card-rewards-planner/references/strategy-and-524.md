# Strategy And Chase 5/24

## Chase 5/24 Basics

Chase is widely reported to deny many applicants for certain cards when they have opened 5 or more personal credit cards across all issuers in the past 24 months. Treat this as an issuer rule of thumb, not a guaranteed official rule.

Count:

- Personal credit cards opened in the last 24 months.
- Authorized-user cards may appear and can affect review; note uncertainty.
- Some business cards do not report to personal credit reports after approval, but inquiries and issuer behavior vary.

Do not count without verification:

- Closed cards outside the 24-month opening window.
- Most loans.
- Most business cards that do not report to personal credit bureaus.

Use exact dates. A card opened on June 15, 2024 generally remains within a 24-month lookback until around June 15, 2026; advise the user to verify report dates.

## Journey Planning Principles

Sequence cards by opportunity cost:

1. Chase cards first when the user is under 5/24 and wants Chase cards.
2. Time-limited elevated offers when value justifies using a slot.
3. Business cards that do not report to personal credit when the user is eligible and comfortable.
4. Ecosystem anchors before niche cards, unless a trip target makes the niche card urgent.
5. Keeper cards before churn-only cards for users who want low maintenance.

Avoid:

- Stacking minimum spends the user cannot meet naturally.
- Opening cards right before major loan applications.
- Using cards with annual fees where credits do not match the user's behavior.
- Burning 5/24 slots on low-value retail cards if the user wants Chase travel cards.
- Ignoring issuer family restrictions and bonus waiting periods.

## Application Path Format

Use this structure:

- Current 5/24 count and dates aging off.
- Goal horizon, such as 3, 6, 12, or 24 months.
- Application timeline by month.
- Minimum-spend windows and exact target dates.
- Expected reward value range.
- Benefits to activate after approval.
- Downgrade, retention, or cancel decision month before annual fee posts.
- Backup cards if denied or if offers change.

## Rule Uncertainty

Issuer rules vary and can change. Clearly label:

- Official terms from issuer pages.
- Widely reported but unofficial rules.
- Anecdotal community datapoints.
- Assumptions that require user verification through credit reports or issuer account pages.
