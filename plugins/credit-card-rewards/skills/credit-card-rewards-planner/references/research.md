# Research

Credit-card offers, rules, annual fees, statement credits, transfer partners, and welcome bonuses change frequently. Browse or otherwise verify current data before making recommendations.

## Source Priority

Use sources in this order:

1. Primary issuer offer pages and terms pages.
2. Official program pages for rewards, transfer partners, credits, and benefits.
3. Reputable comparison or deal-tracking sites for context, historical high offers, and alternative links.
4. Community reports only for unofficial rules or datapoints; label them as anecdotal.

Do not rely only on memory for:

- Current welcome bonus amount.
- Minimum spend requirement.
- Annual fee.
- 0% APR length or transfer fee.
- Statement credit amount or enrollment requirement.
- Transfer partner list.
- Lounge access terms.
- Application restrictions.
- Product-family bonus restrictions.
- Referral, targeted, in-branch, or elevated offers.

## Data To Capture

For each researched card, capture:

- Offer URL.
- Last checked date.
- Bonus terms.
- Minimum spend amount and deadline.
- Annual fee and authorized-user fee.
- Purchase APR or 0% APR terms if relevant.
- Balance transfer fee if relevant.
- Rewards categories and caps.
- Credits and enrollment requirements.
- Key travel protections or purchase protections when relevant.
- Foreign transaction fee.
- Restrictions: once-per-lifetime language, family rules, 5/24-sensitive issuer, product-change issues, and business/personal reporting behavior.

## Offer Quality

When possible, compare the current public offer with historical or commonly elevated offers. Use careful language:

- "Current public offer."
- "Often elevated in the past."
- "Targeted or referral-only, not guaranteed."
- "In-branch reports exist, but verify locally."

## Ranking Method

Do not rank cards by welcome bonus headline alone. Normalize by:

- Realistic point value to this user.
- Minimum spend feasibility.
- Annual fee and credits the user will actually use.
- Opportunity cost under 5/24 or issuer-specific rules.
- Whether the card unlocks future transfer partners or keeper value.
- Complexity and risk of unused credits.

For 0% APR cards, rank by:

- Promotional APR duration.
- Balance transfer fee.
- Whether the promo applies to purchases, transfers, or both.
- Deadline to complete transfers.
- Rewards tradeoff versus dedicated rewards cards.
- Risk of deferred interest or penalty APR terms.
